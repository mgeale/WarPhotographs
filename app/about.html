Frank Hurley

Exhibition of War Photographs

A few months after the Armistice of 11 November 1918 an exhibition took place in Sydney showcasing the photography of Capt. Frank Hurley, an official war photographer for the Australian Imperial Forces.

For many Australians the exhibition was their first visual experience of the devastation the war brought, and it gave graphic detail of life endured by Australian soliders on the frontline.

Titled 'Exhibition of War Photographs', it was held at The Kodak Salon, George St. Sydney, in the early months of 1919.

In this web app I have tried to present the exhibition's photos in the easy to view format, using modern web technologies.

The image files are from The State Library of NSW.
